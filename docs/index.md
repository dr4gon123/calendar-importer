# Calendar Importer

**Calendar Importer** is a Google Sheets add-on that imports events from your
Google Calendar directly into your spreadsheet. Pick a date range, choose the
calendars and columns you need, and click import — no copy-pasting, no manual
formatting.

## What it does

- **Import events into a sheet** — titles, start/end times, all-day flags,
  descriptions, locations, attendees, creator and organizer, status, recurrence,
  Meet links, and calendar links.
- **Flexible date ranges** — quick presets (Last / This / Next × Day / Week /
  Month / Quarter / Year) or custom dates.
- **Choose your calendars** — import from one or many of your Google Calendars.
- **Choose your columns** — pick exactly which fields to export; the sheet is
  built to order.
- **Remembers your setup** — your last date range, calendars, and columns are
  saved for next time.

## Your data and privacy

Calendar Importer runs entirely inside Google Workspace (Google Apps Script). It
reads your calendar events on demand and writes only the columns you select into
the spreadsheet you have open. **Your data never leaves your Google account** — it
is not sent to any server we operate, not shared with third parties, and not used
for advertising or analytics.

The add-on requests three permissions, each used for a specific purpose:

| Permission | Used to |
|---|---|
| Read your Google Calendar (read-only) | List your calendars and fetch the events you choose to import |
| See, edit, create, and delete your spreadsheets | Write the imported events into the active spreadsheet, and remember your preferences |
| Display its sidebar interface | Show the importer inside Google Sheets |

See the **[Privacy Policy](privacy-policy/)** and **[Terms of Service](terms-of-service/)**
for full details.

## Get started

1. Open a Google Sheet.
2. Go to **Extensions → Add-ons → Get add-ons**, search for **Calendar Importer**,
   and install it.
3. Then open **Extensions → Calendar Importer → Open Calendar Importer**, choose
   your date range, calendars, and columns, and click **Import Events**.
